<lore>
Crude oil is useless poured straight into an engine — but distil it, and you get fuels that burn hot enough to power an entire base.
</lore>
<no_lore>
Oil is pumped from the world, distilled into progressively purer fuels, and burned in a combustion engine for high-tier MJ.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Finding & Pumping Oil"/>
Oil generates in lakes and springs, often in deserts or under the ocean. Set a powered <link inline="buildcraft:block/pump"/> above the pool with a pipe or tank to receive the fluid, and it drains the whole deposit. Buffer the crude in a <link inline="buildcraft:block/tank"/>.
<chapter name="Refining"/>
Feed crude into a <link inline="buildcraft:block/distiller"/>. Distillation splits a fluid into a lighter <bold>gas</bold> output and a heavier <bold>liquid</bold> output; running the fractions back through refines them step by step toward pure fuels. The <link inline="buildcraft:block/heat_exchange"/> raises or lowers a fluid's heat between steps, unlocking the hotter distillation stages. Both machines need MJ.
<chapter name="Burning Fuel"/>
Refined fuels burn in the <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_iron"/> — the purer the grade, the more power it yields and the longer it lasts. Keep that engine cooled with water and you have a self-sustaining loop: pump → refine → burn → power the pumps and refinery. The wider power story is in <link to="buildcraft:concept/power_mj"/>.
