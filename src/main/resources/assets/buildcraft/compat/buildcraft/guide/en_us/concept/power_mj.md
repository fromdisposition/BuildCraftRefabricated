<lore>
Power in BuildCraft is a current, not a battery in your pocket. Engines push it down kinesis pipes into whatever machine is thirsty at the far end.
</lore>
<no_lore>
BuildCraft machines run on Minecraft Joules (MJ). Power is produced by engines, carried by kinesis pipes, and consumed by machines on demand.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Engines"/>
The <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_redstone"/> runs on a redstone signal alone — weak, but free and safe; perfect for pulling items from a chest with a wooden pipe. The <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_stone"/> burns solid fuel (coal, charcoal, lava) for steady mid-tier power. The <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_iron"/> burns liquid fuel and is by far the strongest — but it must be kept cool. The <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_creative"/> supplies unlimited power for testing.
<chapter name="Heat & Power Stages"/>
Running engines warm through the stages — <blue>blue</blue>, <green>green</green>, <yellow>yellow</yellow>, <red>red</red>, then black. Hotter means faster, with more power per pulse. The Redstone Engine simply stalls when hot, but a fuel-burning engine (Stone or Combustion) will <red>explode</red> when it overheats — keep the Combustion Engine fed with coolant, and never let an engine's power back up with nowhere to go. Always point an engine's piston face directly at the machine or kinesis pipe it should feed.
<chapter name="Moving & Converting Power"/>
Carry MJ with kinesis pipes — see <link to="buildcraft:concept/power_transport"/>. To bridge other energy systems, the <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_rf"/> and <link inline="buildcraft:block/mj_dynamo"/> convert between Forge Energy (FE/RF) and MJ.
