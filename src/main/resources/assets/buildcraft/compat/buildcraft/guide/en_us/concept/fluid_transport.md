<lore>
Fluids don't ride inside pipes like items — they fill them, flowing toward wherever there is room.
</lore>
<no_lore>
Fluid pipes move liquids — water, oil, fuel, lava — between machines and tanks.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Fluid Pipes"/>
Build liquid lines from <link inline="buildcraft:pipe/wood_fluid"/>, <link inline="buildcraft:pipe/cobble_fluid"/> and the other fluid pipes. The wooden fluid pipe extracts liquid from a tank or machine when powered, exactly like its item cousin. Higher-throughput pipes carry more per tick, and the <link inline="buildcraft:pipe/iron_fluid"/> acts as a one-way valve so fluid can't flow back.
<chapter name="Storage"/>
Buffer liquids in a <link inline="buildcraft:block/tank"/>. Stack tanks vertically and they merge into one tall, see-through reservoir, so you can watch the level rise.
<chapter name="Flood Gate"/>
The <link inline="buildcraft:block/flood_gate"/> pours a fluid out into the world from its open faces — drain a tank of water to irrigate a farm, or place lava deliberately. Pair it with a <link inline="buildcraft:block/pump"/> elsewhere to relocate whole lakes. To carry mechanical power instead of fluid, see <link to="buildcraft:concept/power_transport"/>.
