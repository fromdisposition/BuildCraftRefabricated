</no_lore>
<chapter name="Templates vs Blueprints"/>
A <bold>template</bold> records only the <italic>shape</italic> of a structure — which spaces are filled — to be rebuilt from any block. A <bold>blueprint</bold> records the exact blocks, their orientation, and even some contents. You create both at the <link inline="buildcraft:block/architect"/>: frame an existing build with markers and let it scan.
<chapter name="Building"/>
Load a template or blueprint into a <link inline="buildcraft:block/builder"/>, give it power and the required materials in adjacent inventories, and it constructs the design in the world. Store, copy and rename your designs with the <link inline="buildcraft:block/library"/>.
<chapter name="Fillers"/>
For bulk shapes that don't need a blueprint, the <link inline="buildcraft:block/filler"/> fills a marked volume with a chosen pattern — walls, floors, pyramids, clear-outs and more (see <link to="buildcraft:concept/filler_patterns"/>). Mark out the regions these machines work on with land and volume markers — see <link to="buildcraft:concept/markers"/>.
