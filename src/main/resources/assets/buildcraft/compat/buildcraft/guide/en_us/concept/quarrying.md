<lore>
The Quarry is the machine that made BuildCraft famous: mark an area, supply power, and walk away while it strip-mines to bedrock.
</lore>
<no_lore>
BuildCraft's mining machines dig automatically — from a single shaft to whole chunks — and deposit the loot into your pipes.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Mining Well & Pump"/>
The <link inline="buildcraft:block/mining_well"/> drills straight down a single column, leaving a shaft you can climb. The <link inline="buildcraft:block/pump"/> does the same for liquids, draining lakes of water, oil or lava into a tank or pipe.
<chapter name="The Quarry"/>
The <link inline="buildcraft:block/quarry"/> mines an entire marked area down to bedrock. Surround the region with land marks (up to 64×64), place the Quarry beside one and power it; it first builds a frame, then a head travels inside it, mining every block as though with a diamond pickaxe. Output flows into any adjacent inventory or pipe.
<chapter name="Marking & Output"/>
Define the dig area with markers — see <link to="buildcraft:concept/markers"/>. Pair the Quarry with a wooden pipe and chest, or a full sorting network (<link to="buildcraft:concept/item_transport"/>), so cobble, ores and gravel each go where you want. As always, more power means faster mining.
