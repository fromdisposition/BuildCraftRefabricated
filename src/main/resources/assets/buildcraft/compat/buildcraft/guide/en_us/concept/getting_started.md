<lore>
Welcome, engineer. BuildCraft is about machines that work while you don't — pumping oil, chewing chunks down to bedrock, and tying it all together with pipes and logic.
</lore>
<no_lore>
BuildCraft adds automated mining, item and fluid logistics, oil refining, and programmable logic. This page is a road map — follow the links to learn each system.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="First Steps"/>
Almost everything begins with a <link inline="buildcraft:item/gear_wood"/>. Craft wooden gears, then stone, iron, gold and diamond — most BuildCraft recipes climb this ladder. Gears build your first machine and your first engine.
<chapter name="Power"/>
Machines run on <bold>Minecraft Joules (MJ)</bold>: engines produce power, kinesis pipes carry it, machines spend it. Begin with a cheap <link inline="buildcraft:block/engine_redstone"/>, then move up to combustion once you can make fuel. The whole system is explained in <link to="buildcraft:concept/power_mj"/>.
<chapter name="Where To Go Next"/>
Dig automatically with the <link to="buildcraft:concept/quarrying"/> guide. Move goods around with <link to="buildcraft:concept/item_transport"/> and <link to="buildcraft:concept/fluid_transport"/>, and carry power with <link to="buildcraft:concept/power_transport"/>.
Refine crude into fuel in <link to="buildcraft:concept/oil_and_fuel"/>. Make your base think for itself with <link to="buildcraft:concept/gates_and_wires"/>, and copy whole structures with <link to="buildcraft:concept/builders_and_blueprints"/>.
For late-game automation, see <link to="buildcraft:concept/silicon_and_chipsets"/> and <link to="buildcraft:concept/robots_and_zones"/>.
