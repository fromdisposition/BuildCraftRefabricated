<lore>
Markers are the surveyor's stakes of BuildCraft — you plant them to tell machines exactly where to work.
</lore>
<no_lore>
Markers define the regions and paths that quarries, builders and fillers operate on.
</no_lore>
<chapter name="Land Marks"/>
A <link inline="buildcraft:block/marker_volume"/> projects a beam when you right-click it; place several and they snap into a box up to 64×64×64. That box becomes the area a Quarry mines (see <link to="buildcraft:concept/quarrying"/>), a <link inline="buildcraft:block/filler"/> fills, or an <link inline="buildcraft:block/architect"/> scans.
<chapter name="Path Marks"/>
A <link inline="buildcraft:block/marker_path"/> chains into a path rather than a box, used to lay out routes for machines that follow a line. Link a set of marks together quickly with the <link inline="buildcraft:item/marker_connector"/>.
<chapter name="Tidy Up"/>
Marks are only construction guides. Once a machine has read its region you can break the marks — the machine remembers its area until it is reset.
